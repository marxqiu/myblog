Q2 — Solar Panel Clearance

Q4 (Bonus) — Docking Clearance
Phase 2 correct:
C = 800 mm, D = 750 mm
C ≥ D? 800 ≥ 750 → YES
Margin = 800 − 750 = 50 mm = 5 cm ✓
Phase 1 likely wrong:
C read as "80 mm", D = "750 mm"
80 < 750 → FAILS ✗ (reversed result)

DEBRIEF NOTES  |  Q1 is the most dramatic: the stack fits exactly (0 m clearance) when units are correct — but is 456.5 m long without conversion. Use this to ask: "How did engineers not catch this for 10 months?"
Q2 produces "5000 m solar panels" — use for humour. "Would those panels fit inside this building?"
Q4 reverses the pass/fail result entirely — collar looks fine without units, looks dangerously narrow with them. Good for discussion of how errors can go in either direction.
